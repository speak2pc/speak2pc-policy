Privacy Policy

Speak2PC (package: com.speak2pc.app) · Effective date: July 5, 2026 · Last updated: July 5, 2026

Summary in plain words Speak2PC turns your speech into text and sends that text directly from your phone to your own computer over your local network. We (the developer) do not operate any server, do not collect your voice or your text, and cannot see what you dictate. Speech recognition is performed by your device's speech service (typically provided by Google), which is governed by its own privacy policy.

1. Who we are

Aayesha Pathan ("we", "us") publishes the Speak2PC app ("the App"). Our website is speak2pc.github.io/speak2pc-policy. For any privacy question or request, contact us at help.speak2pc@gmail.com.

2. What the App does with your data

2.1 Microphone and speech

  • The App accesses your microphone only while you actively use dictation (after you tap the microphone button and grant the microphone permission).
  • Your speech is converted to text by your device's system speech recognition service (on most devices this is provided by Google). When online recognition is used, audio is transmitted to that provider's servers for processing. When the App's "on-device / offline" option is enabled and supported by your device, recognition is performed locally on your phone.
  • The App itself does not record, save, or upload audio. No audio ever reaches any server operated by us.

2.2 Transcribed text

  • The text produced from your speech is sent directly from your phone to the computer you configured, over a direct local connection between the two devices (shared Wi-Fi network, phone hotspot, or USB), using the address you entered or scanned via QR code.
  • This transfer is device-to-device. The text does not pass through, and is not stored on, any server operated by us.
  • If your computer is unreachable, unsent text may be temporarily stored on your device only so it can be delivered later. You can delete it at any time.

2.3 Data stored on your device

To function, the App stores the following information locally on your phone. None of it is transmitted to us:

  • App settings (selected language, toggles such as smart formatting, verbatim mode, text size);
  • the IP address of your computer that you entered;
  • your saved quick phrases;
  • learned corrections: short word pairs derived from edits you make to dictated text, used only to improve your future dictation accuracy on your device;
  • daily usage counters (dictation time and word counts) used to enforce in-app usage limits;
  • temporarily queued messages that could not yet be delivered to your computer.

2.4 Camera and QR pairing

The App can use your camera to scan a QR code displayed by the companion program on your computer, purely as a shortcut for entering connection details. Scanning happens entirely on your device; the App does not take, save, or upload any photo or video, and the camera is used only while the QR scanner screen is open.

2.5 Optional AI features (your own Gemini API key)

The App includes optional AI-powered features (for example, AI voice commands) that use Google's Gemini API. These features are off by default and work only if you choose to enter your own Gemini API key:

  • Your API key is stored only on your device. It is never sent to us, and we have no access to it.
  • When you invoke an AI feature, the relevant text or command is sent from your device directly to Google's Gemini API using your key. That processing is governed by Google's own terms and privacy policy (see Gemini API Terms and the Google Privacy Policy).
  • We do not receive, log, or store the content you send to or receive from the Gemini API.
  • You are responsible for keeping your API key confidential and for any usage or charges incurred on your Google account through your key. If you believe your key has been compromised, revoke it in your Google AI Studio / Cloud console. You can remove the key from the App at any time in its settings.

2.6 Text from photos (OCR)

The App can extract text from a photo you choose from your gallery. This happens entirely on your device using on-device text recognition:

  • Only the image you explicitly select is accessed; the App does not browse, scan, or index your photo library.
  • The image is processed locally and is never uploaded to us or to any server as part of this feature. The App does not keep a copy of the image.
  • The extracted text is treated like any other text in the App: you can edit it and send it to your own computer, as described in section 2.2.

2.7 What we do NOT do

  • We do not require or create user accounts.
  • We do not collect, store, sell, or share your personal data.
  • We do not show third-party advertising.
  • We do not use third-party analytics or tracking SDKs.

2.8 Purchases (if offered in the future)

The App is currently free. If we later offer paid features or subscriptions, payments will be handled entirely by Google Play Billing. Google processes the transaction and provides us only with non-payment information needed to unlock what you bought (such as purchase status and order tokens). We never receive your card number or full payment details. Google's handling of payment data is governed by the Google Privacy Policy.

3. Permissions the App requests

PermissionWhy it is needed
Microphone (RECORD_AUDIO) To capture your speech for dictation while you use the mic. Never used in the background.
Network / Internet (a) To let the system speech service perform online recognition; (b) to send transcribed text directly to your own computer over the connection you choose — shared Wi-Fi network, phone hotspot, or USB connection.
Camera (CAMERA) Used only to scan the QR code shown by the companion program on your computer, to set up the connection quickly. Camera frames are processed on your device in real time for QR detection only; no photos or videos are captured, stored, or transmitted.

4. Third-party processing

Speech-to-text conversion is performed by the speech recognition service installed on your device, most commonly Google's speech services. Their processing of your audio is governed by their own terms and privacy policy, for example the Google Privacy Policy. Enabling the App's offline/on-device recognition option (where supported) keeps recognition on your phone.

5. Data retention and deletion

  • All App data listed in section 2.3 lives on your device and remains there until you delete it.
  • You can delete it at any time by: using the App's built-in reset/wipe option; clearing the App's data in Android Settings; or uninstalling the App.
  • Because we hold no copy of your data, there is nothing for us to delete on a server. If you nevertheless contact us with a deletion request, we will confirm this and assist where possible.

6. Security

Text is transmitted only within your own local network to the computer you designate. You are responsible for the security of your local network and of the receiving computer. We recommend using the App on trusted, password-protected Wi-Fi networks.

7. Children

The App is not directed at children under 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your jurisdiction), and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information through the App, contact us at help.speak2pc@gmail.com.

8. Your rights

Depending on where you live (e.g., under the GDPR in the EEA/UK or similar laws elsewhere), you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or restrict processing of personal data. Because the App stores data only on your own device and we collect none, you can exercise these rights directly through your device as described in section 5. For anything else, contact us at help.speak2pc@gmail.com.

9. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the App evolves. The "Last updated" date above will change, and material changes will be noted in the App's store listing or release notes. Continued use of the App after an update means you accept the revised policy.
